Large number of potential photon/rhodopsin quantal responses:   $N$

Small probability of success for each potential response:     $p$

Mean number of `successful' quantal responses in each trial:    $\mu = pN $

$\mu \lll N$      →      We can use Poisson statistics

Poisson statistics

Let $\mu$ be the average number of quantal responses per flash

Poisson statistics gives the probability that the flash yields exactly $k$ quanta: \[ P_k = \frac{\mu^n e^{-\mu}}{n!} \]

The Probability of Seeing is the probability that the number of quanta exceeds a threshold $n$: \[ P_{see}=\sum_{k=n}^{\infty} P_k \]
